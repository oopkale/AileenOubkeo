Case Study



ThredUp

ThredUp is most effective in saving money, raising awareness through their millions of engaged customers, and saving pounds of carbon emissions being released into the atmosphere.

In 2009, the co-founder & CEO James Reinhart realized he had a closet full of clothes he didn’t wear. He wasn’t alone. He knew there was value in those clothes, and “looking back at that moment, I certainly didn’t appreciate how that insight could eventually upend how we look at innovation in retail, the apparel industry, and our environment.”


Most Effective Method

Donate Clothes Through “Kits” From ThredUp

The business offers an option to donate and have a system where you pick up a “kit” and fill it with gently used clothing and then drop it off at a post station. It is more efficient and convenient where people don’t have to drive far to donate.



Ultimately, the most effective way to combat fast fashion is to help make it easier for people to donate or get rid of their clothes in an environmentally friendly way.


Shop from stores that make more sustainable and higher quality clothes

Big clothing company names try to take advantage of trends to maximize profits and will produce large amounts of clothing in a short amount of time and as a result, they will make clothes with low quality. It is better to shop at stores with higher quality clothes and invest your money in clothes that you are likelier to wear for several years.

initial research

Need Statement

People need to find more sustainable ways to shop and get rid of clothes by donating and shopping at thrift stores that way, companies are not making more cheap clothing.

Sources

    Problem Statement

    Fast fashion is damaging to the environment because it consumes thousands of gallons of water to make clothes that are not made to last in order to meet consumer demand.

    Evidence

    Impact

  • According to “The Impact of Fast Fashion on the Environment” it takes 3 thousand liters of water to produce a single cotton shirt and 20% of the waste water globally is from the water used to dye each shirt.
  • The clothes that are made are with low quality and will wear out within a few washes. In 2019, 62 million metric tons of apparel were consumed.
  • The fashion industry is responsible for a fair amount of carbon emissions released each year. If they maintain the course they have been taking now, an increase of 50% in carbon emissions is expected to rise.

    • Perspective

  • H&M is well known for its contribution to fast fashion and making clothes that are cheaply made. According to Doctor Christina Dean, founder and CEO of Redress has commented that despite H&M for being caught contributing to fast fashion, "we should not just blame H&M and Bestseller, as they have been the only ones that got caught, while luxurious brands are the ones that even burn leather."
